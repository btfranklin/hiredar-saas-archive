How Hiredar Works for Recruiters

An under-the-hood look at the ingestion, matching, and reporting stack.

A transparent, practical overview—no buzzwords, just how it actually works.

Step 1

Bulk Intake and Validation

You can drag-and-drop individual files or upload a ZIP. We place the resumes into a named pool you control and screen out system artifacts and unsupported file types before processing.

  • Uploads are checked for size and format so bad files don’t waste your time.
  • Background workers process each resume so you can keep working while it runs.
  • We never alter your originals; temporary working copies are removed after processing.

Step 2

From Resume to Structured Profile

We extract the text from each resume and turn it into a structured candidate profile—skills, experience summary, qualifications, and basic contact details—so you can scan and compare consistently.

  • Text is pulled from PDFs and Word files and standardized to reduce formatting noise.
  • Key details are organized into fields you can search and sort.
  • A concise, recruiter-facing tagline summarizes strengths for quick triage.
  • If a resume lacks basic signals (like a name or skills), we skip it rather than clutter your pool.

Step 3

Matching and Ranking Across Multiple Lenses

When a job is active, we compare its requirements to your candidate pools and calculate scores from several angles, not just keywords.

  • Separate lenses highlight skills fit, relevant experience, qualifications, wildcard alignment, and an overall “holistic” score.
  • Low-signal results are filtered out by threshold so your list stays focused.
  • Open a match to see a plain‑English explanation of why the candidate fits that role.
  • As you add resumes or activate jobs, rankings refresh automatically in the background.

Step 4

Shortlisting and Shareable Reports

Add candidates to a shortlist with a click, then export clean CSVs or presentation‑ready PDFs when you’re ready to share with hiring managers.

  • Shortlists show the headline, scores by lens, and key details at a glance.
  • CSV exports make it easy to analyze in spreadsheets; PDFs are formatted for manager review.
  • Credits are checked and deducted on export, so usage stays predictable and transparent.
